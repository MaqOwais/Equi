## What is Equi?

Equi is an **AI-driven, research-based bipolar disorder monitoring and stabilisation system** built to address one of the most under-served public health crises in the United States. Over 6 million Americans live with bipolar disorder ‚ÄĒ yet most go years without a correct diagnosis, consistent monitoring, or care that understands the full picture of their cycles.

Equi closes that gap. Every feature is designed around the clinical reality of cycling between mania and depression: continuous monitoring, AI-generated reports, wearable data integration, and optional direct access to licensed US psychiatrists.

> **Prescription features are optional.** Equi works fully as a standalone monitoring tool ‚ÄĒ cycle tracking, journaling, AI reports, and all other features function without any psychiatrist connection. Connecting with a psychiatrist and receiving prescribed activities is an additional layer for those who want it.

> *"Not a wellness app. A clinical-grade monitoring system that fits in your pocket."*

## Core Features

<br/>

### ūüĆä Cycle Tracker
Track mania, depressive, mixed, and stable states with daily symptom checklists and intensity ratings. Visualised as a 90-day wave graph. AI detects patterns ‚ÄĒ *"You've entered depressive episodes 3 days after sleep drops below 5h."*

### ūüďď Notion-Style Journal
Block-based editor with text, checklists, mood scales, and cycle tags. Every entry is auto-tagged with your cycle phase, sleep score, and activities completed that day. View any day's full picture in the unified calendar. All journal blocks use a portable format ‚ÄĒ plain text is extracted cleanly for calendar display and AI reports.

### ūü§Ė AI Wellness Reports
Weekly and monthly AI-generated summaries correlating sleep, mood, journal tone, activity completion, and heart rate. Generated by Llama 3.1 70B via Groq's zero-retention API. Exported as a PDF to share directly with your psychiatrist before appointments.

### ūüéĮ Activity Library
18+ therapist-backed activities filtered by cycle phase ‚ÄĒ *"good for depressive days"*, *"good for stable days"*. Three tabs: **All** (filtered by phase), **Prescribed** (activities your psychiatrist has assigned), and **Working for Me** (your personal bookmarked toolkit). Each activity has **individual reminder notifications** ‚ÄĒ set a custom time and choose whether a notification ring plays, all managed per-activity from the Activities section of your profile.

### ūüĎ• Community
Anonymous posts, no algorithmic feed, no likes. Channels: *Wins This Week, Depressive Days, Mania Stories, Medication Talk, Caregiver Corner*. Crisis hotline always pinned at the top. Every post is validated by an AI moderation pipeline (Perspective API + Llama 3.2 3B) before going live ‚ÄĒ it flags (never silently removes) and always gives the user a path to edit or escalate.

### ūüŹ• Psychiatrist Marketplace
Browse and book licensed, bipolar-specialist US psychiatrists for in-person or telehealth sessions. Search by location with a map view. Equi Partner psychiatrists can prescribe activities directly and receive your AI reports. Booking is integrated via Calendly. Share your wellness report before each appointment with one tap.

### ūüďÖ Unified Calendar
Every data point ‚ÄĒ journal entry, mood log, cycle state, medication adherence, substance use, activities, and workbook entries ‚ÄĒ visible on a single day view. Days with workbook entries are marked with a gold dot. Export supported.

### ūüÜė Crisis Mode
One tap from anywhere in the app. Shows your saved emergency contacts, local crisis lines, and immediate grounding tools (5-4-3-2-1 Grounding, Box Breathing). A persistent modal that can be summoned from the tab bar, never more than one tap away.

### ūüďė Bipolar Workbook
A comprehensive evidence-based CBT & DBT workbook with **7 sections and 49 prompts**, fully drawn from clinical psychoeducation frameworks. Each prompt has three layers: a main question, a clarifying sub-question, and a collapsible **How it helps** explanation grounded in clinical research (psychoeducation, Social Rhythm Therapy, DBT, ACT, narrative therapy). Multiple timestamped entries per prompt ‚ÄĒ your reflections accumulate over time rather than being overwritten. Includes an appendix of evidence-based techniques (5-4-3-2-1 Grounding, Box Breathing, Early Warning Signs Checklist, Crisis Plan Template, STOP Skill, Opposite Action). Responses are private and never shared without your explicit consent.

**Sections:**
1. Understanding Your Bipolar Patterns
2. Exploring Emotional Triggers
3. Cultivating Healthy Routines
4. Strengthening Emotional Regulation
5. Building Supportive Connections
6. Crisis Prevention and Safety Planning
7. Long-Term Recovery and Meaningful Living

### ūüóďÔłŹ Daily Routine Builder
Customisable daily checklist and journal prompts. Set your own habits (morning walk, sleep time, screen limits) and track completion each day. Feeds directly into the AI Wellness Report.

### ūü•ó Diet & Nutrition Tracking
Daily food quality check-in ‚ÄĒ not a calorie counter. Three-tap "Eating well?" log on the Home screen. For deeper detail: photo snap (AI identifies food quality), voice logging, or barcode scan (Nova ultra-processed classification via Open Food Facts). Patterns feed into the AI Wellness Report as a dedicated NUTRITION & MOOD section. Never mandatory. Never calorie-focused.

### ūüö¨ Substance Use Monitoring
Simple daily check-in for alcohol and cannabis ‚ÄĒ the two substances most documented to destabilise bipolar cycles. One tap per substance, no judgment framing. Patterns feed into the AI Wellness Report.

### ūüíú Well-wisher & Guardian Access
Friends, family, and caregivers use the same Equi app with a "Supporting someone" role. Guardian mode gives caregivers automatic alerts when high-risk thresholds are triggered and the ability to help manage the account during a crisis. All sharing is per-person and always revocable.

### ūüēź Social Rhythm Tracker
Log your daily rhythm anchors ‚ÄĒ wake time, first meal, first social contact, work start, dinner, bedtime ‚ÄĒ each day. Based on IPSRT landmark RCTs: stabilising the timing of these routines directly reduces relapse in bipolar disorder. A Rhythm Consistency Score in the AI Wellness Report flags when irregularity is increasing.

### ūüíä Medication Adherence
One-tap daily check-in: Taken / Skipped / Partial. Optional side-effect log (fatigue, weight changes, tremor, cognitive fog). Adherence patterns feed into the AI Wellness Report. Visible to your psychiatrist only with explicit per-person consent. Conditional ‚ÄĒ only shown to users who are on medication.

### ūüĒé Relapse Signature Builder
A guided setup where you document your personal early warning signs ‚ÄĒ the specific things that happen first when heading toward a manic or depressive episode. The AI cross-references your journal and cycle data against your signature and flags early pattern matches.

### ūüďĆ Life Events Log
A dedicated block type in the Journal for logging events that disrupt daily routines. Life events appear as markers on the 90-day wave graph, allowing the AI to surface correlations between disruptions and subsequent mood episodes.

## Data Architecture

All core features are **offline-first**: journal entries, mood logs, activity completions, and workbook responses are saved locally first and synced to Supabase when connectivity is available. A write queue (`lib/offline-queue.ts`) handles deferred sync.

**Key Supabase tables:**
- `profiles` ‚ÄĒ extends `auth.users`, stores cycle state, onboarding step, diagnosis
- `journal_entries` ‚ÄĒ block-based entries with cycle/sleep/activity metadata
- `activity_completions` ‚ÄĒ per-user completions with cycle state, notes, bookmarks
- `workbook_responses` ‚ÄĒ per-prompt responses with `chapter`, `prompt_index`, `entry_date`; multiple entries per prompt allowed
- `prescribed_activities` ‚ÄĒ psychiatrist-assigned activities with dosage and frequency
- `medications` ‚ÄĒ medication list with adherence log
- `community_posts` ‚ÄĒ anonymous posts with AI moderation status
- `psychiatrists` / `bookings` / `psychiatrist_connections` ‚ÄĒ marketplace and connection management

**Row Level Security (RLS)** is enabled on all tables. Users can only read and write their own data. Psychiatrists access patient data only through the explicit connection consent flow.

## Safety Flags

Equi is a clinical-grade app for a population with documented vulnerability to app-induced adverse events. Five evidence-based risks are built into the design from the start.

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Mood monitoring ‚Üí rumination | **High** | Low-mood safety circuit (‚Č§3/10 for 2+ days) ‚Üí optional coping prompt; one log per day max |
| Positive activities ‚Üí hypomania induction | Medium | Cycle-phase gating: Gratitude Jar, Compliment Diary, Proud Dandelion removed from recommendations during manic phase |
| Behavioural activation without anti-hypomania guardrails | Medium | Phase-aware Home suggestions; phase restriction field in Psychiatrist Portal |
| Clinical framing of grounding / mindfulness | Low | Honest evidence labelling in activity descriptions ‚ÄĒ no extrapolated bipolar claims |
| Data privacy and stigma risk | **High** | Zero AI data retention (Groq); no third-party analytics SDKs; plain-language privacy UX; immediate data deletion |

## Design Principles

1. Never use red except for genuine crisis/emergency UI
2. Mood states have colors ‚ÄĒ they are never labeled "bad" or "good"
3. No streaks that punish missing days ‚ÄĒ Equi celebrates returning, not consistency
4. All community posts are anonymous by default
5. No algorithmic feed ‚ÄĒ community is chronological only
6. Psychiatrist data is never shared without explicit per-person consent
7. Offline-first ‚ÄĒ core features work without internet
8. Your data is always exportable and deletable
9. Never calorie-focused (food quality only ‚ÄĒ 11 categories)
10. Zero AI data retention (Groq zero-retention API)
